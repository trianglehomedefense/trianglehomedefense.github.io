Correct the access points wildlife use.

Inspection-driven exclusion protects the home by identifying weak points and correcting them with practical, structure-aware work.

The process

Visible, accessible, documented.

The first job is to understand the structure. We look for openings, rub marks, chew damage, droppings, nesting material, movement paths, and vulnerable transitions. Then we explain what we can see, what we cannot confirm without further access, and what correction options make sense.

Inspect.
Accessible exterior, roofline, vents, foundation, crawlspace, attic indicators, and problem areas reported by the homeowner.
Prioritize.
Separate active/likely entry points from general vulnerabilities so the scope stays realistic.
Correct.
Seal, screen, reinforce, repair, or refer when broader structural, moisture, electrical, insulation, or contractor work is needed.
Common correction areas

Where animals usually find opportunity.

Roofline and trim gaps

Returns, soffits, fascia, rake boards, trim transitions, and chew-prone edges.

Vents and utility penetrations

Gable vents, exhaust vents, crawlspace vents, pipe gaps, and utility line openings.

Foundation and crawlspace edges

Door gaps, access wells, loose screens, block transitions, low trim, and debris pressure.
