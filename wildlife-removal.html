Wildlife problems start with access.

We inspect the home, document visible evidence, and explain what conditions are allowing animal activity.

Inspection-first

Wildlife service without scare tactics.

The focus is not the animal alone. We look for the route, shelter pressure, and structural condition behind the problem.

Squirrels

Roofline gaps, soffit/fascia damage, chew points, attic indicators, and repeated entry risk.

Birds

Vent nesting, ledges, openings, and areas where screening or exclusion may be appropriate.

Snakes + rodents

Foundation gaps, crawlspace access, utility penetrations, debris pressure, and movement routes.

Bats

Likely entry points, timing concerns, staining, rub marks, and documentation before action.

Scope note: Final recommendations depend on species, access, safety conditions, season, applicable restrictions, and what is visible at the time of inspection.

Field documentation

Read the structure before choosing the fix.

Animal activity often follows shelter, gaps, weather damage, or repeated weak points. We document what is accessible and separate likely entry points from general vulnerabilities.

  1. EvidencePhotos, staining, rub marks, droppings, sounds, damage, and homeowner reports.
  2. AccessRoofline, foundation, crawlspace, vents, utility lines, siding channels, sheds, and trim gaps.
  3. PlanPractical corrections, prevention options, safety limits, and next steps.
